# Taking Payment
[TOC]

We can accept payments by cash and card. We are able to offering cashback on card transactions
Only 1 till has a cash drawer. If the customer is paying by cash, you’ll have to move to this till to process the payment.

## Cash Payments
Take the money from the customer all in one go. Don't let them change their mind once they give you the cash.

!!! warning "Scams"
	One of the most common scams involves confusing staff about the notes they have been given, so the customer ends up walking away with more in change than they handed over in the first place.

If you’re unfamiliar with UK currency, please tell the bar managers: we can show you all the various notes and coins and explain how to tell good notes from bad ones.

To put the money in the till, enter the quantity you’ve been given and press "Cash". For example, if given £10.50, you would press "1 0 5 0 Cash". The till will work out and display any changes that are due.

If you’re given a single note, you can press the button for it, £5, £10 or £20 as appropriate. If you’re given the right amount of money, you can just press "Cash" without entering the amount.

!!! warning "£50 Notes"
	We can accept £50 notes but please ask a to check it first due to the risk of fakes.


If the money you were given isn’t enough to cover the drinks in the transaction, putting it in the till won’t close the transaction. The till will show the payment followed by the total still to pay, and you can ask the customer for more cash or ask them to pay by another method. You can enter as many payments against a transaction as necessary until it is paid up and closed.

### Valid Tender
All UK tender is now made of a plastic material, older paper notes are not valid any more.
You can see pictures of the [current notes on the Bank of England website.](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/banknotes/current-banknotes)

## Card Payments
The card terminals are fixed to the front of the bar, facing the customers. Each terminal has a name shown on your side. Pick the terminal you will use and direct the customer towards it.

On the till, press "Card" and pick the terminal you chose from the menu.

The terminal will display the amount due and allow the customer to pay. Once the customer has paid, this will be shown on the till, and the transaction will close automatically.

If you need to cancel the payment (for example, if the customer decides to buy something else as well), press Cancel on the till. The customer can also cancel the payment from the card terminal. If the payment is cancelled, this will be shown on the till, and the transaction will stay open.

!!! warning "Watch out for transaction clashes"
	Ensure the card transaction is completed successfully before moving on to the next customer.


## Multiple/Split Payments
Sometimes, customers want to pay for drinks in unusual ways, such as by putting amounts on several different cards. The till lets you enter the amount before pressing the payment button.

Example: A transaction comes to £30.65. The customer wants to put £10.00 on 1 card £20.00 on another and give you 70p in cash.

1. Enter "1 0 0 0 Card" on the till, and the customer can make the first payment.
The till will show the card payment and £25.65 left to pay.

2. Enter "2 0 0 0 Card", and the customer can make the second payment.
The till will show the second card payment and £0.65 left to pay.

3. The customer gives you 70p in cash: you enter "7 0 Cash".
The till shows that £0.05 change is due and closes the transaction.

## Receipts
If a customer requests a receipt, you must move to the till, which has a cash drawer and receipt printer.

You can print the current transaction for a customer by pressing the Print button. If the transaction is open, it will only print a list of items entered so far rather than a tax receipt. If the transaction is closed, it will print a tax receipt.

If a customer wants a receipt but tells you too late and you have already started another transaction, please tell a bar manager who can help.
