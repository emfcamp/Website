# Keg Taps

There are 6 keg beer lines running to the bar. There are 2 banks of 6 taps; you can serve from either bank of taps.

!!! warning "Hygienic pouring technique"
Do not allow any part of the tap to come in contact with the cup or the drink when pouring.

All of the taps have a flow control lever on the side. Once this is set up for a particular beer it shouldn’t need to be moved. If you think a beer is flowing too fast, too slow, or is too frothy, please talk to a bar manager about it and **don’t try to fix it yourself!**

## Procedure
The procedure for serving from keg taps is:

1. Hold the cup ready in 1 hand at a 45º angle ready to “catch the beer”.

2. Turn the tap on fully for just a fraction of a second until the initial foam has poured into the drip tray.

3. Quickly move the cup under the spout.
You should start at 45º so the beer will run down the back of the cup.

4. If the drink is too frothy, please speak to a bar manager, do not adjust the flow control lever yourself.

5. Gradually rotate the cup to vertical as it fills past halfway.

6. Close the tap.

7. Put the full cup on the counter.

## Foamy Pints
If there is too much foam and you can’t make the beer to the rim/line, there are 2 options depending on the type of drink:

If the foam disappears quickly, set it aside for 30 seconds and then top it up again, remembering to use the technique in step 1 above. This minimises wastage.

For drinks where you know the foam sticks around for longer, continue pouring, but tilt the cup backwards slightly and let the foam overflow into the drip tray until you reach the rim or line.

If this is consistently happening, let the bar manager know so that it can be adjusted.

## Empty Keg
When a keg is empty, the flow should be cut off cleanly. In this case, you don’t have to throw away the part-filled cup: let the bar manager know, and they should be able to change the keg; once that’s done, you can finish filling the cup. When the flow stops, close the tap so it doesn’t start up again on its own!

If there’s a problem with the beer (it’s too frothy, it tastes bad, or it’s run out) you can record this using the display above the tap, and the bar managers will take a look at it.
