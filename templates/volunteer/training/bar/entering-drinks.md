# Entering Drinks
[TOC]

Most of the buttons on the till keyboard correspond to drinks. When you press a button, the drink is added to the current transaction. It also records the drink out of that particular cask, so we always know how much we’ve sold and how much is remaining.

The same applies to drinks like spirits; the button on the till is for the type of spirit, and the till knows exactly which bottle we’re serving from. When we reach the end of a bottle, we tell the till the reference number of the next bottle going on sale. Doing this lets us figure out how much we’re wasting, how much we’re selling, and how much we have left.

## Numbered Taps, Pumps and Ciders
The button tap, pump and cider buttons refer to the physical taps, pumps and boxes where drinks are served. The till knows which cask, keg or box is connected to which button and, thus, what price we’re selling it for. 

So if a customer asks for Five Points Pale on Tap 2, press "Half Tap 2" or "Pint Tap 2" as appropriate for a half pint or whole pint.

## Specifying Quantities
A single press of a button will enter a single drink. 

There are 3 ways to enter multiple drinks:

1. Just press the same drink repeatedly.

2. Enter a number, press the "Quantity" button, and then press the button for the drink.
This can be quicker for drinks with sub-menus like wines and soft drinks.

3. Modifier keys are underlined and have a cream background colour. These adjust the quantity of the product. The till usually has "Case", "Double", "Carton" and "Half" modifiers that look like this:
<img src="/static/images/bar/till-case-button.png" width="100px">

When selling an entire case of Club Mate, you can hit "Case" and then "Club Mate" for the whole box.

You can also use this for a 1-litre carton of orange juice by pressing "1l Carton" then "Juice Mixer", then "1", which is orange juice.

You can sell double spirits by pressing Double, then the spirit, such as Amaretto.

## Cancelling a Line
If you hit the wrong button, don’t panic! You can use the up and down arrow keys to highlight the wrong drink and then press the "Cancel" key to remove it.

If you do this immediately after entering the wrong drink, it will disappear. If you wait a while before using the "Cancel" key, it will add another entry to the transaction, reversing the line for the wrong drink.

The till keeps a running total of the cost of the drinks in the transaction and shows it all the time at the bottom of the list on the screen.

## Discount for Cup Reuse
All drinks sold include the price of the cup hire, customers are asked to return the cup to us as loss or damage will cost EMF over 50p per cup.

If the customer has reused one of our cups or brought their own, you may record 1 discount per cup reused. A transaction can mix and match new and reused cups by simply recording only the reused cups.

To record a reused cup discount, simply press the "Cup Reuse" button for as many cups as have been used before the customer pays.

This discount improves EMF's sustainability and helps us record data for further sustainable efforts in future years.
