# Licensing Law

- You **must not** serve alcohol to a customer if they are or appear to be drunk.

- You **must not** serve alcohol to anyone who is under 18 years old.

- You **must** know and follow the age verification policy.

!!! danger "You can receive a £90 on-the-spot fine"
	If you are caught serving alcohol to people who are drunk or underage by officers of the police or local licensing authority, you may be given an on-the-spot fine of £90.
	
	The EMF organisers may face a potentially unlimited fine.


## Weights and Measures

We must serve accurate measures of alcohol to comply with licensing laws that are designed to help people regulate their consumption of alcohol.

At EMF beer is served in multiples of half a pint (284.131ml). The pint (568.261ml) and half-pint cups we use are CE or UKCA marked and legal to serve directly.

If you serve beer using a customer's cup, it must be marked with a CE or UKCA mark. If it does not state “(half) pint to line” it is (half) pint to the brim. 

Wine is served in measures of 125ml, 175ml and 250ml. The cups aren’t marked for this, so we have CE-marked 125ml and 175ml thimble measures behind the bar.

Spirits are served in multiples of 25ml at EMF. We have CE-marked thimble measures and optics (where you push the cup up to serve an exact measure).

!!! warning "European cup sizes"
	Be aware that European customers may have CE marked 500ml cups which cannot be used as this would be less than the legal measure.
