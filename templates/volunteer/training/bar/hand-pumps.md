# Hand Pumps

We have 4 hand pumps, which will be used for serving cask ales. They have short spouts with flip-top control valves. We'll demonstrate serving beers with these when you start working on the bar.

!!! warning "Hygienic pouring technique"
	Do not allow any part of the tap to come in contact with the cup or the drink when pouring.

## Procedure
The procedure for serving from hand pumps is:

1. **Hold the cup under the spout**
You should start at 45º so the beer will run down the back of the cup.

2. **Open the valve**

3. **Pull the handle gently towards you**
You don’t need to pull hard; there’s a limit to how quickly the beer will flow along the pipe, and if you pull too hard, you’ll just create a vacuum in the pump cylinder.
One pull on the handle should dispense about half a pint of beer.

4. **Push the handle back**
Repeat pulling until the cup is full. Gradually rotate the cup to vertical as it fills past halfway.

5. **Close the valve**

6. **Put the full cup on the counter**
When a cask runs out, you’ll serve froth and air. You’ll have to throw it away and tell the customer to pick something different.
Please tell the bar manager when a cask runs out so they can clean the line and put another cask on it.
