# Customer Safety
When it’s busy behind the bar, it’s easy to get fixated on the rush of serving customers quickly and overlooking someone beyond the bar taps who might need our help.

We might need to help someone if they’re drunk, someone is harassing or touching them, they’re arguing or even just asleep.

This might seem hypothetical, but like most health and safety issues, it’s “written in blood.” Unfortunately, it happens all too often, and our job behind the bar is to look out for vulnerable customers and find help for them.

## What to do if someone needs help
If you see someone who might be in an unsafe situation, it’s our job to act.

1. Stay with them, don’t leave them for a second.
2. Bring them behind the bar if you can.
3. Tell the bar managers you need help with an Angel Shot, or:
	- Call from any event phone:
    	- Bar managers: 2277 
		- First aid: xxxx
		- Security: xxxx
	- Radio for assistance by holding the push to talk button and saying “[Main Bar / Cybar] calling [Security / First Aid], we have [urgent] incident <insert brief description>, help is required.” then wait for a response.

## Angel Shot / Ask for Angela
As well as watching how our customers behave, they can come to us and ask for help. Some might use a code word or ask something that sounds strange.

If someone asks for an Angel Shot or asks for someone called Angela, they’re trying to ask for help without someone else knowing. They may feel unsafe or have experienced some kind of violence.

You should continue the ruse that this is a normal conversation while subtly seeking help from another person so you can stay with the customer.
