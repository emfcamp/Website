# Volunteer Handbook - First Aid

**Do not place yourself in danger** - the first priority in case of emergency is
to avoid making things worse. Don't let yourself become an additional casualty.

**Do not call 999** - there is an ambulance crew on site who can
reach you faster than 999 can.

In most cases people who need first aid should make their own way to the first aid tent, which can be found near the bar. If they are unable to do so:

1. Ensure they are not in immediate danger. If they are, and you can do so safely, get them to safety.
2. Notify First Aid via radio, or on extension 1099 for emergencies. (1090 for anything that isn't life threatening).

Unless you're qualified to do so, or the person is in imminent danger, you should not attempt to perform first aid yourself. We have paramedics on site who will be with you as soon as possible.
