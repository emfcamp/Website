# Volunteer Handbook

Thank you for signing up to volunteer! Before starting your first shift please
familiarise yourself with the contents of this handbook. It contains information
on how to volunteer, and guidance on how to respond to incidents you might be
informed of while volunteering.

## How to volunteer

Our volunteer system splits the work that needs to be done to make EMF happen
into roles, each of which has a number of shifts across the event. In most cases
you can pick any shift that doesn't have enough volunteers yet and sign up to
fill it. In a few cases you'll need to complete a training session before you
can take shifts.

1. Select some [roles](/volunteer/choose-roles) you're interested in.
2. Complete any [training](/volunteers/training) required.
3. Register for some [shifts](/volunteer/schedule).
4. Check in when you arrive, either with a team lead or via the self check-in QR code.
5. Do your shift.
6. Check out when you leave, again with a team lead of via self check-in.
7. [Enjoy a meal from the volunteer kitchen!](./food)

## Important contacts

If someone asks you for help, or something seems wrong, and you don't know what
to do speak to your team lead if they're nearby. If they're not you can consult
this list to find the best team to speak to, a phone that can call these extensions
can be found in all structures, or you can use a DECT phone if you have one.

| Team | Phone | What they can help with |
|---|:---:|---|
| First Aid | 1090 | People who are injured or physically unwell. |
| Welfare | ? | People who need mental health support. |
| Help Desk | 1001 | Information for attendees, lost property, and generally helping people out. |
| Volunteer Desk | 1009 | Guidance on your shift, the next volunteer not relieving you. |
| Conduct | 1234 | Code of Conduct complaints |
| Security | Radio | Incidents of violence, or if you believe someone is at risk of violence |
| Event Operations | 1000 | Any non-emergencies not covered above |
| Duty Site Manager | 1010 | Emergencies |

## Emergencies & Vulnerable People

In case of emergency or being informed of vulnerable people (particularly lost
children or vulnerable adults) please follow the guidance below. Even if you are
familiar with processes from other events please keep to this guidance, which will
ensure we can respond as effectively as possible.

In all cases remember the following:

**Do not place yourself in danger** - the first priority in case of emergency is
to avoid making things worse. Don't let yourself become an additional casualty.

**Do not call 999** - we're usually equipped to provide help faster than a 999 operator
can. If emergency services are required we will call them.
